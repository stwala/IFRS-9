The evolution of credit loss accounting standards

Post-Crisis Reform

Post 2008 financial crisis, regulators realized history-based accounting standards could be misleading, necessitating a paradigm shift in methodologies.

IASB Standard

In July 2014, IASB issued IFRS 9 to replace IAS 39, introducing a forward-looking approach to address delayed credit loss recognition.

Global Implementation

Effective globally in Jan 2018, IFRS 9 posed challenges for firms unfamiliar with predictive analytics or expected loss modeling.
